# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

AI builders, coding-agent users, indie hackers, product engineers, reviewers, and teams that generate rich outputs during software work. They arrive from a build session, a shared agent result, or a developer workflow, often needing to preserve, inspect, and share an artifact without turning it into a full project.

## Product Purpose

Artifacts gives agent-generated work a durable home. HTML reports, Markdown specs, JSX prototypes, PR writeups, research pages, and small tools get permanent URLs, immutable versions, previews, access control, and one shared authorization model across web, CLI, REST, and MCP. Success means generated work stops disappearing into temp folders, chat attachments, or one-off uploads.

## Brand Personality

Precise, agent-native, and durable. The brand should feel like a technical spec sheet with a crafted object at its center: quiet, confident, version-aware, and built for people who trust tools that expose their mechanics.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS gradients, purple-on-white AI landing pages, decorative glassmorphism, vague productivity claims, oversized metric blocks, and endless icon-card grids. Do not make the page feel like a docs template with the product name swapped in, and do not hide the product behind abstract AI language.

## Design Principles

1. Show the artifact lifecycle, not a generic promise.
2. Make agents and humans feel like equal first-class users.
3. Treat durability, versioning, and authorization as visible product primitives.
4. Prefer crisp technical structure over hype.
5. Use the logo and Geist Pixel as memorable brand artifacts, not decoration everywhere.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible navigation. Respect reduced motion. Keep body copy short and readable, maintain clear focus states, and avoid communicating state through color alone.
